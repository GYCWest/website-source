Each year GYC West hosts a young adult retreat to gain strength in the faith, network safely with like-minded believers, and seek a closer communion with our Father. For 2023 the theme is "Irresistible Influence." More details about this event and the theme will come soon.

Join us in 2023 to come away from your work and routines and seek revival together.

Cheyna Ashe

Cheyna is originally from New York and has spent most of her life on the east coast. In 2016, she made her way to the west coast, where she currently resides in Sacramento, California. Cheyna has always had a very special interest in music. She has two albums made public (“Finally Free” and “Increase My Faith”), with her newest album, “Gratitude,” in the works. She has found her ultimate purpose in using her God-given passion for music to spread God’s truth worldwide!

K'dee Crews

Dr. K'dee Crews is a licensed Clinical Psychologist at Beautiful Minds Medical in Northern California. After work hours, she enjoys sharing the gospel in diverse ways, including through public speaking, teaching and supervising aspiring counseling students, hosting a mental health podcast (“The Brain People”), and hosting a YouTube series on mental health and the Bible (MANNA Ministry: “Truth Prescriptions”). Her ultimate joy is sharing how the truth of a compassionate God can heal our broken world, lives, and hearts.

Stephen Gutierrez

Stephen has had the privilege of working in pastoral ministry, literature ministry, and other forms of evangelism in different capacities. He is currently pursuing his MA in Pastoral Ministry through Andrews University and works as the Evangelism Assistant for Streams of Light International. He and his family live in Northern California.

Anil Kanda

Anil Kanda is the Senior Youth/Young Adult Director for Central California Conference. He comes from a Hindu/Sikh background, but over 20 years ago, Christ touched his heart. Anil authored the book, “Rethinking God” and has traveled worldwide sharing the good news of Jesus Christ. You will be blessed by his energetic and thought-provoking presentations!

Hannah & Jerry Rasmussen

Hannah grew up in the beautiful mountains of British Columbia and was blessed to be raised by SDA parents who value nature as an expression of the Creator's love. She currently lives in Washington State where she works with a midwifery practice and birth center that provides pregnancy, birth, and mother-and-baby care for low-risk women. She still enjoys the simple beauties of nature as a revaluation of the Creator, Who uses every possible means to bless and draw His children to Him. Her favorite Bible verse is Hebrews 10:23, “Let us hold fast the profession of our faith without wavering; (for he is faithful that promised;).”

Jerry Rasmussen, Hannah's father, was raised in a pastor's home in northern California surrounded by the beauty and awesome grandeur of the Sierra-Nevada Mountains. At the early age of five he felt drawn to work for the Lord Jesus Christ. As a teenager, he realized that God has no grandchildren and he chose to recommit to Jesus and make this relationship his own. His love of the ocean and tide pooling was cultivated while living near Trinidad (California Coast). He feels blessed when he finds himself in a white water raft or canoe, on a mountain top, or at a tide pool. In 1986, after receiving his teaching certificate from Pacific Union College, he taught science, Bible, and math at a variety of Academies in California, Montana, and is currently teaching in British Columbia, Canada. His primary focus has been to inspire young people with the joy of discovery and realizing their uniquely personal potential as missionaries for the Master. He enjoys helping others learn about God's love, power, and trustworthiness through His Word and created works.

This year's GYC West is being held at Albion Field Station, mere walking distance from the Pacific ocean.

34000 Albion St, Albion, CA 95410

A note about GPS: Google Maps has been proven accurate, however the last bit where you drive off of Highway 1 onto Albion Ridge Rd then onto Albion St can be a little difficult to see - it caught many of us the first time! When you turn off of Highway 1, pull into the parking lot you see in front of some small storefronts. Off the north-east end of the lot are two rough roads, the right fork is the one you are looking for - you'll see a little blue-on-white sign for the Albion Field Station. We'll have GYC West signage out where possible.

Expect temperatures in the 50° to 70° range, with refreshingly moist ocean winds and lots of sun.
